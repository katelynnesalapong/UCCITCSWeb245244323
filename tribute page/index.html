About Benjamin R. Salvosa

In the aftermath of World War II, Benjamin Romero Salvosa envisioned Baguio City as an “educational capital of the North.” His vision led to the founding of Baguio Colleges on June 19, 1946 an institution that would later become the University of the Cordilleras.

Salvosa believed that education could rebuild not only lives but communities, shaping generations of learners in the Cordillera region and beyond.

His dedication and faith in learning live on through UC’s continued excellence and its role in nurturing future leaders.
